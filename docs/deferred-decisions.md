# Dao Tree — Deferred Decisions

*Compiled 2026-07-02; Wes's review pass applied same day. Each entry: what's parked, why it matters, the lean, and — where marked **DECIDED** or **REFRAMED** — the outcome of the review. This file is the living ledger (the old design docs live in git history — `git show 5887814:docs/<name>`).*

**Review-pass structural notes (Wes):** #8 and #11 are duals — the chronicle makes the past legible as narrative, the veil makes the future illegible as mystery; together they state one posture ("memory accretes, foresight is earned" — the emotional shape of cultivation fiction). ## Design decisions — worth deciding before or during slice 9 (Act II)

1. **Aspect routes are lattice-locked.** Element Soul Aspects require a held lattice Seed, so sect-focused and pill-focused builds are structurally confined to Formless. **REFRAMED (review pass):** the lock is fine ONLY if sect and pill each own something equally identity-defining — otherwise they're not builds, they're flavors of "not-lattice," and breaking the lock would paper over the real gap. Before acting on the cluster ratio, name the counter-monopolies. Working proposal to test against the run: **lattice owns WHO you are** (aspect, identity), **sect owns STANDING** (institutional multipliers, arsenal, deeds), **alchemy owns WHEN** (timing control — burst windows, breakthrough amplification, tribulation insurance). The diversity run now attributes sect/pill lag to the Formless penalty specifically (counterfactual probes) so we don't break the lock chasing the wrong cause. **RUN RESULT (2026-07-02): the lock costs these builds NOTHING — the counterfactuals came out SLOWER (metalSoul is insightMult-only, strictly worse than Formless's balanced 1.2/1.2 for a Qi-banking build). The lag is systemic: SectFocused is the weakest grammar (40.7h, 1.98× Competent) on its own merits. So the aspect lock stands unaccused; the open item is now purely the sect's counter-monopoly (what does standing buy that nothing else can?). The metalSoul trap-aspect side finding was **FIXED same day (562c6ad)**: all Seed-gated aspects now carry the Formless floor on both axes plus their lean, lint-pinned ("no earned aspect punishes the default grammar"); counterfactual probes verify zero cost. **SECT ROUTING DECIDED (review pass 2): do NOT read 40.7h as "sect is weak" and do NOT de-sublinearize the contribution mults — the sim measures time-to-capstone and structurally undervalues non-temporal payoffs. Sect's question routes through #8/#10: if sect becomes where the inhabited-world systems live (chronicle, rivals, reputation), it's the tortoise that owns the story and SHOULD pace slower — an axis the sim can't price. Flip condition: if #8/#10 land elsewhere and sect owns nothing distinct, then it's not a tortoise, it's under-tuned, and the contribution mults get the pass.**

2. **Keep-rule topology gaps (found by the competent-actor sim).** No keep rule ever protects `c` (Core Formation climb), and the `foundationSurvivesNascentSoul` keep is immediately re-wiped by each cycle's own c-climbs — so late-game play re-climbs c forever. ~30% of the competent actor's n-climb time is this churn. Decide: add a c keep rule (e.g., n milestone), accept the churn as intended texture ("the core must be re-tempered each ascent"), or fold into Act II's keep design. *Lean: decide inside slice 9's keep-rule work, not before.* **REFRAMED (review pass):** treat as design opportunity, not cleanup. The 30% c-churn is a rationalized emergent mechanic; when slice 9 touches keeps, the choice is deliberate — DELETE the churn (add a c-keep; churn was tax) or make the re-temper FELT (visible hardening, compounding payoff; churn becomes ritual). Same code touch, opposite philosophies. Do not sleepwalk into the delete. **ESCALATED (review pass 2): #13 is BLOCKED on this decision** — the Realistic 53.25h and this item are the same finding (the churn inflates Competent ~3h and Realistic worse, since it both re-does and forgets). Decide tax (c-keep: both numbers drop, pin the lower figures) or ritual (churn stays, 53h is real, make it felt): two philosophies, two different pins. **PROBE LANDED (2026-07-02, † counterfactual runs in `npm run sim`): Competent 20.57h → 5.61h with a c-keep (churn tax 14.96h, 72.7% of the entire run); Realistic 53.25h → 30.25h (tax 23.00h, 43.2%).** For an optimizer, the c-re-climb ritual IS most of the game's length. The Realistic−Competent gap (32.68h) splits 75.4% genuine human imperfection (idle cadence) / 24.6% extra churn from keep-topology. So the stakes of this call are larger than the original ~30% estimate: tax → Act I optimizer floor becomes ~5.6h (short); ritual → the churn needs to carry weight proportional to being ~73% of an optimal run. A middle path exists (partial keep — e.g. c milestones survive but best doesn't, softening re-climbs without deleting them) and would need its own probe. Decision is Wes's; slice 9's keep-rule work is where it lands.

3. **Corruption threshold ratchet.** Crossed heart-demon thresholds never re-arm: bleed corruption to zero and the old trials don't re-fire; the ladder only ratchets upward (then repeats its final trial every +120). Intended reading: each demon is faced once, then only the deepest returns. Alternative: cyclical trials (re-arm on full bleed-out) for farmability. *Lean: current ratchet is correct — Dao Heart farming already exists via the repeat ladder; re-arming would make bleed-out a farming exploit.* **DECIDED: ratchet stays.** (But see #6 — the ratchet's farmability is now an input to Samsara's economy.)

4. **Tribulation banked-Qi fuel vs automation.** The preparedness pool's banked-Qi term rewards holding a large pile at trigger time, while auto-q-prestige (Automation Tier 1) continuously spends the pile. Currently self-resolving in practice (the maturity model rests when formed), but nothing *guarantees* automation never eats a tribulation bank. Carried from the TMT-era review as "acceptable v1, flag for design." *Lean: leave until a real player report.* **DECIDED: leave — with the reframe that if the guard is ever built, build it for FEEL (the game holds its breath while the tribulation is ready; automation pausing reads as reverence) and the bank-safety comes free.**

5. **"Qi gains from spending" expedition modifier.** The design doc's Inverted Spirit Land example needs spend-path hooks the engine doesn't have; v1 shipped an Insight-driven inversion instead. The idea is recorded in `secret-realm.ts`'s header as a modifier family (gain-from-spending, cost-from-time, progress-from-stillness) for future sites. *Lean: build the spend-hook plumbing when Act II adds its realm sites, not before.*

## Decisions scheduled for slice 10 (Samsara)

6. **Dao Heart stacks across reincarnation.** Currently life-scoped; lean was eternal. **REFRAMED (review pass, coupled to #3):** eternal + the repeat-ladder's farmability = unbounded meta-currency across lives. That is either the demonic fantasy fully expressed (unboundedness as that build's intended endgame) or the thing Samsara soft-caps across lives — but it is THE question Samsara's economy must answer explicitly, not a side effect to discover. The whole samsaric economy balances around this answer; decide it first in the slice-10 design, before any Samsara numbers are written.

7. **Seclusion rungs across reincarnation.** Shipped eternal by design intent ("QoL is never clawed back") — Samsara's implementation must honor it; recorded so the slice-10 implementer doesn't "helpfully" reset them. *Not really open — a confirmation checkpoint.*

8. **Chronicle / epitaph system** (horizons §2). **REFRAMED (review pass): bound to #11 as one narrative-spine design pass** (the chronicle makes the past legible; the veil makes the future illegible — duals of a single posture). And consciously scope-decided BEFORE building: the "data table + formatter" costing is the floor, and anchoring on it risks shipping a formatter where the ceiling is a pillar — the dynasty-history save is the design's most distinctive retention hook (the screenshot-a-tragic-biography moment the research pointed at). The Samsara slice must decide flavor-line vs. system explicitly; the cheap version forecloses the tall one.

## Strategic (timing matters more than the answer)

9. **Steam intent.** **DECIDED (review pass): soft yes — architect for it, commitment to launch deferred.** Rationale: the option value decays fastest of anything on this list; save-migration and achievement scaffolding are cheap to build in and expensive to retrofit, and Act II is about to multiply what would need migrating. Consequences now: (a) golden-save lineage harness in CI before slice 9 content lands (a save from each shipped version, loaded + ticked through migrations); (b) achievements modeled as a registry that can map to Steam achievements later; (c) settles #15 (README matters now) and #13 (the Realistic band is the number to polish).

10. **Rivals-as-weather** (horizons §3). **DECIDED (review pass): split at the cheap/expensive seam.** Ambient rivals ship (another cultivator's tribulation lights the horizon, a breakthrough shakes the sky — no interaction; most of the inhabited-world payoff at a fraction of the cost); interactive rivals (contested slots/resources) are a v2 pillar, not built now. Architect the ambient events so interaction could hook in later. Xianxia is among the most social fantasy genres — pure solitude leaves its biggest hook on the table, but the interactive version balloons scope.

11. **Knowledge-veil / ancestral knowledge** (Wes's planned system). **RECUT (review pass): the axis is not aggressive-vs-gentle timing — it is WHAT gets veiled. Veil the AHEAD, never the NOW.** Grades are the language the optimizer thinks in; hiding the current loop's grades withholds the player's own controls and reads as hiding the ball (incremental players are optimizers first). Veiling is powerful on flavor-critical, optimization-optional information: the nature of unreached realms, the names of things not yet touched, what Spirit Severing is actually like. Mystify the horizon; keep the current loop fully legible. Bound to #8 in the narrative-spine pass.

## Technical debt (recorded, not urgent)

Next hint-grammar touch should pay this down: one `not:` combinator retires most shadow keys. (Noted in `meets.ts`.)

13. **Pacing bands — pin after calibration.** Three bands, three jobs. **CALIBRATION LANDED (2026-07-02), numbers awaiting Wes's sign-off:** cluster ratio **1.418** raw / 1.578 aspect-adjusted (focused builds 28.7–40.7h); Competent regression floor **20.57h**; Realistic experience target **53.25h** — ABOVE the inferred 25–40h band (driven by late-game 1800s check-in idle + every-other-cascade core restoration; sensitive to the `REALISTIC_*` ⟨tune⟩ constants). **DESIGN CALL MADE (review pass 2): 53h to the capstone is fine — no pacing review.** (44% of the reference band, Melvor-scale for a long-tail idle, loops-with-closures the whole way up; the 2.59× Competent-to-Realistic ratio is the design-pure sweet spot — optimization rewarded, not mandatory.) **BUT the number is NOT pinned: #13 is blocked on #2** (the c-churn share must be decomposed out first — pinning 53.25 today bakes a fixable inefficiency into the Steam-planning figure) **and on the jitter sweep. BOTH PROBES LANDED (2026-07-02):** churn decomposition in #2; jitter sweep (9-point grid: late check-in 1440/1800/2160s × bank factor 1.3/1.5/1.7) gives **min 48.55h / median 53.25h / max 62.35h, swing 1.284× — WIDE, so Realistic pins as a RANGE [~48.5h … ~62.4h], not a point.** Secondary sweep finding: the banking-factor knob has zero measurable effect (check-in quantization dominates — the idle pile always overshoots every threshold within one interval), so the experience band is a one-knob function of session cadence. Right shape for an idle game, but the pinned band is really a session-cadence assumption. Sequencing unchanged: decide #2 → re-run Competent + Realistic → pin all three bands (Realistic as a range).
