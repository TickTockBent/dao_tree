Before acting on the cluster ratio, name the counter-monopolies. Working proposal to test against the run: **lattice owns WHO you are** (aspect, identity), **sect owns STANDING** (institutional multipliers, arsenal, deeds), **alchemy owns WHEN** (timing control — burst windows, breakthrough amplification, tribulation insurance). The diversity run now attributes sect/pill lag to the Formless penalty specifically (counterfactual probes) so we don't break the lock chasing the wrong cause. **RUN RESULT (2026-07-02): the lock costs these builds NOTHING — the counterfactuals came out SLOWER (metalSoul is insightMult-only, strictly worse than Formless's balanced 1.2/1.2 for a Qi-banking build). The lag is systemic: SectFocused is the weakest grammar (40.7h, 1.98× Competent) on its own merits. So the aspect lock stands unaccused; the open item is now purely the sect's counter-monopoly (what does standing buy that nothing else can?). The metalSoul trap-aspect side finding was **FIXED same day (562c6ad)**: all Seed-gated aspects now carry the Formless floor on both axes plus their lean, lint-pinned ("no earned aspect punishes the default grammar"); counterfactual probes verify zero cost. **SECT ROUTING DECIDED (review pass 2): do NOT read 40.7h as "sect is weak" and do NOT de-sublinearize the contribution mults — the sim measures time-to-capstone and structurally undervalues non-temporal payoffs. Flip condition retired.

2. **Keep-rule topology gaps (found by the competent-actor sim).** No keep rule ever protects `c` (Core Formation climb), and the `foundationSurvivesNascentSoul` keep is immediately re-wiped by each cycle's own c-climbs — so late-game play re-climbs c forever. ~30% of the competent actor's n-climb time is this churn. Decide: add a c keep rule (e.g., n milestone), accept the churn as intended texture ("the core must be re-tempered each ascent"), or fold into Act II's keep design. *Lean: decide inside slice 9's keep-rule work, not before.* **REFRAMED (review pass):** treat as design opportunity, not cleanup. The 30% c-churn is a rationalized emergent mechanic; when slice 9 touches keeps, the choice is deliberate — DELETE the churn (add a c-keep; churn was tax) or make the re-temper FELT (visible hardening, compounding payoff; churn becomes ritual). Same code touch, opposite philosophies. Do not sleepwalk into the delete. **ESCALATED (review pass 2): #13 is BLOCKED on this decision** — the Realistic 53.25h and this item are the same finding (the churn inflates Competent ~3h and Realistic worse, since it both re-does and forgets). Decide tax (c-keep: both numbers drop, pin the lower figures) or ritual (churn stays, 53h is real, make it felt): two philosophies, two different pins. **PROBE LANDED (2026-07-02, † counterfactual runs in `npm run sim`): Competent 20.57h → 5.61h with a c-keep (churn tax 14.96h, 72.7% of the entire run); Realistic 53.25h → 30.25h (tax 23.00h, 43.2%).** For an optimizer, the c-re-climb ritual IS most of the game's length. The Realistic−Competent gap (32.68h) splits 75.4% genuine human imperfection (idle cadence) / 24.6% extra churn from keep-topology. So the stakes of this call are larger than the original ~30% estimate: tax → Act I optimizer floor becomes ~5.6h (short); ritual → the churn needs to carry weight proportional to being ~73% of an optimal run. **DIRECTION DECIDED (2026-07-02, Wes): partial keep — "the core remembers."** The decomposition's magnitude disqualifies both clean options from opposite sides. Tax is out: a 3.7× collapse of the optimizer floor to 5.6h makes Act I a prologue, and worse, it proves the churn was *load-bearing structure* — nothing that is only a tax accidentally becomes three-quarters of optimal play; deleting it kicks out a wall and relocates the rebuild into slice 9 at higher cost. Pure ritual is out: it asks one static felt beat ("the core hardens, visibly") to survive 73%-of-run repetition, and no single sensation does — the forge-glow that moves at hour two is wallpaper by hour twelve. The partial keep is not a hedge; it is the only reading consistent with the magnitude: **the re-climb IS the game, and the acceleration curve across re-climbs is Act I's felt progression** — not "I broke through" as an event but "the mountain I climb every ascent is getting shorter." Probe requirements (in flight): (a) **report the curve, not the total** — a uniform discount is just a smaller tax; acceleration at the same hours is the design (structural note: c has only 3 substages, all latched before the first cascade, so milestones-only keep predicts a FLAT discount; an explicit per-ascent compounding term is the probable real mechanic — probe both); (b) **both actors** — a partial keep barely moves Realistic's total (75% is cadence) but changes the texture of the churn quarter the human sits through; judging by total would misread it as doing nothing; (c) **legibility is the whole ballgame** — the second pass must visibly outrun the first at the exact moment tax-instinct says "I already did this," or partial-keep degrades into ritual-with-extra-steps. **Fallback pre-named:** if the curve comes back structurally fine but THIN (too few re-climbs inside Act I to be felt), the fix is Act II's Spirit Severing inheriting and extending the curve across acts — not more churn, not abandoning the middle path. Slice 9's keep-rule work is actually about this.

    **WES'S READING OF THE PROBE (2026-07-02 — the governing frame for slice 9's mechanic):** (1) **The mechanic, named:** not "retained churn with a feel-good frame" — "**re-tempering the core fuels the ascent, and mastery means the fueling gets faster.**" The literal xianxia trope (the early cultivator re-stabilizes for days; the advanced one does it in a breath, and the re-stabilization itself generates the feedback powering the next breakthrough) — and the sim showed it's also the math. (2) **Pin r by the Realistic CURVE SHAPE, not the total** — the total is fine anywhere in 0.65–0.80; the feel is in the shape. The failure mode to check: a tail of effectively-instant climbs makes the ritual INVISIBLE — "a vanished ritual is just full-keep with extra steps; a brief ritual is 'I still do this, but I've mastered it'" — one tick of felt engagement per cycle is the difference. If fixed r vanishes the tail, the fix is a scale that flattens toward a floor (asymptote to "very fast," never zero). **FINE SWEEP LANDED (2026-07-02, "R REFINEMENT" section in `npm run sim`): the vanish worry does NOT materialize at any r in the neighborhood.** Realistic's own imperfection provides a natural floor — the multi-cascade lapse-recovery segments keep re-inflating the tail (k=6/8/10 span 4–5 cascades), so even r=0.65 bottoms at ~1.6–7.5min climbs, zero "instant" climbs anywhere; true vanishing needs r=0.5. Verdicts: **BRIEF at r ∈ {0.65, 0.70, 0.75} and r=0.7+f=0.05; HEAVY (not yet a ritual) at r=0.8 and r=0.7+f=0.1.** At r=0.7 the last four climbs run 3.5/14.5/5.1/3.6min with 9/12 single-breath — mostly mastered, occasionally still real. Notable texture: the curve is two interleaved accelerating classes — routine re-tempers (5400→2520→…→214s, monotone) and post-lapse recoveries (3328→1482→872s, also monotone) — so recovery-from-neglect ALSO gets mastered, and the occasional bigger re-temper after a lapse is thematically exact (neglect the core, pay again — briefly). The floor choice is register, not rescue: plain r=0.7 tail ≈ a breath (3.6min); f=0.05 tail ≈ a moment (9min). Wes picks the register in slice 9; everything in [0.65, 0.75] is structurally sound. (3) **The Competent floor is demoted to a regression instrument, not a pacing claim.** The optimizer already self-accelerates; r barely changes its picture (6.58h vs full-keep's 5.61h); the floor collapse was never the player's experience. The number defended at Steam-planning time is the REALISTIC band. This dissolves the earlier "5.6h = prologue" concern — the load-bearing argument, not the floor number, was what disqualified tax. (4) **12 re-climbs is enough resolution**: supports "steep then plateau," which is the right complexity for a mechanic whose job is "feel mastery"; more shape would be overdesign. (5) **Pin migration once r is final (slice 9):** Realistic re-pins at [~28–35h] cadence-labeled (width still the session-accommodation feature); Competent re-pins wherever it lands, as instrument; cluster re-checked with r in place. (6) **Process, banked:** the finding was RELATIONAL — r=0.7 tested alone against base would have read "faster, good"; only the four-variant × two-actor comparison revealed the optimizer/human inversion and that the middle path is a third thing outperforming both poles, not a compromise between them. Multi-variant probes earn their cost.

    **Reading instructions for the probe results (Wes, 2026-07-02):** (a) **r is a genre decision, not a tuning knob** — r=0.85 says the mountain stays imposing (the game respects the obstacle), r=0.5 says the tenth climb is a victory lap (the game celebrates distance traveled); cultivation fiction does both at different scales, so the honest answer may be **r as a decaying function of k** (early re-climbs accelerate fast, late ones plateau into "you've mastered this, the challenge is elsewhere"). Failure signature of any fixed r: late climbs trivially fast OR early ones indistinguishable from full cost — either means r must be a function. Read the curve shape, not the total. (b) **Re-climb count is a resolution constraint**, not just a thin-curve warning: seven re-climbs can support "noticeably faster each time"; they cannot support "fast, plateau, fast again." Match curve complexity to count. (c) **The ascent counter is architecture**: active state that accumulates across resets and feeds back into the climb — metaprogression BELOW the prestige layer, the second such accumulator after Dao Heart. Slice 9 decides one-off vs. first member of a "the body remembers" family; the counter's data shape should accommodate siblings either way. (d) **If the thin-curve fallback fires**, the counter survives the tribulation — a third "survives tribulation" item, which is the trigger to invert the framing: define what the tribulation RESETS and let everything else persist by default (slice-9 architecture question, forced by the fallback, not the probe).

## Parked design directions

17. **Celestial weather / the almanac** (Wes, 2026-07-02 — idea parked, couplings recorded). Semi-predictable celestial cycles — alignments, comets, seasons of qi — that modulate progression differently per path, changing through a game-year in a learnable pattern. Couplings, all load-bearing: (a) **shares the ambient-world substrate with #10** — a world-clock/almanac emitting scheduled + seeded events is the natural architecture of "ambient rivals with hooks for later interaction"; rivals' tribulations could themselves be almanac-scheduled ("during the Crimson Comet, someone attempts ascension"). Architect once, not twice. (b) **This is the pre-named mechanic that wakes the dead banking knob (#13)** — an alignment worth banking toward makes banking discipline matter again, so the cadence-labeled Realistic band WILL move when this lands; the label makes that legible and expected, not mysterious. (c) **Tension with the counter-monopoly triad (#1): alchemy owns WHEN.** Weather handing timing-control to every path for free dilutes alchemy's identity axis. Resolution lean: weather is the *world's* schedule (exogenous), alchemy remains the *player's* timing (endogenous) — and alchemy gets the deepest weather interactions (brewing under alignments, bottling a comet's qi, harvest windows), turning the tension into alchemy's upgrade. (d) **#11 fit: never veil the now, veil the ahead** — current sky-state and its effects fully legible; *forecasting* is earned horizon content (diviner techniques, sect astrologers, ancestral star-records) — the knowledge veil's most natural member. Design rules if built: **opportunity-shaped, never obligation-shaped** (no dead sky — alignments rotate which path waxes; rare events leave harvestable residue so the sleeper is never punished, only the surfer bonused); **deterministic seeded almanac** (pure function of world-time + seed — sim determinism survives); **weather-ignorance bounded** (a choice-viability assertion: ignoring the sky entirely costs at most X% — surfing is optimization reward, never mandatory, per the 2.59× philosophy). Sim cost is real but tractable: actor policies for surf/ignore + a weather-off counterfactual probe, same attribution discipline as always. Timing: NOT slice 9 (already loaded); design inside the #8/#10/#11 narrative-spine pass, build alongside ambient rivals on the shared almanac.

    **EXPANDED (2026-07-02, Wes — the loot frame):** Samsara changes what rarity IS. In a single-run frame, a rare 10× event creates permanent unearned inequality (FOMO); in a loop frame it's **loot** — "one more run, this time I'll get that herb." So the system is TWO tiers, two amplitudes, two emotional registers: (a) **the predictable cycle** (seasons, new moons, eclipses, harvests, migrations) at moderate amplitude (1.3–2×) — the texture layer; rewards planning, creates check-in context, and is what sect divination reads; (b) **rare spawns** (the thousand-year herb, the Wandering Sage encounter, the once-in-a-lifetime alignment) at dramatic amplitude (3–10×) — the loot layer; rewards presence and creates stories. Wakes the banking knob as pre-named in #13: the Realistic band becomes two-knob (cadence × timing), and the difference between catching and missing an auspicious window is interesting rather than punitive when the amplitude tiers are respected. Weather also completes the anti-static-beat design for the core re-climb (#2): acceleration is the deterministic variation, weather the semi-stochastic one — the tenth re-climb is both faster than the first AND happening under a sky the first never saw; the two sources multiply.

    **The rules (Wes, binding on any implementation):**
    - **Loot, never gate.** Base completion can NEVER require a rare event — the tribulation is reachable every life, every build, with zero rare spawns. Rare events change a run's CHARACTER, not whether it finishes ("the ginseng makes a life extraordinary; its absence makes a life ordinary, not failed"). **Enforced in the sim:** Competent runs with rare spawns disabled and must stay inside the pinned band — if it can't, the events are hidden requirements, not loot.
    - **Aggregate-certain, specific-rare** (the slot-machine math): every life has near-certain probability of encountering SOMETHING unusual, low probability of the specific thing hoped for. A completed life where literally nothing unusual happened is a system FAILURE — not because loot was needed, but because the run lacked texture. Testable: seeded multi-life sweep asserting P(≥1 rare) ≈ 1 per life while per-event rates stay low.
    - **Determinism discipline:** seeded almanac (predictable tier = pure function of world-time) + per-life seeded encounter table (loot tier). The sim runs fixed seeds; a seed-sweep probe bands the encounter variance the way the jitter sweep banded cadence.
    - **Lean (mine, for Wes's review): rare spawns roll on ENGAGEMENT, not wall-clock.** Predictable-tier windows are wall-clock (forecastable, plannable — missing one is a planning outcome); loot-tier events fire on check-in/action rolls, so a sleeping player can't miss what only spawns in their presence. That kills residual FOMO dead while preserving the jackpot register — the elder is met, not scheduled.
    - Chronicle coupling (#8): the loot layer is what fills the chronicle with DIFFERENTIATED content — "in my third life I met the Wandering Sage at early Core Formation" is a story precisely because another player's third life lacked it. The chronicle must record encounter provenance from day one of the loot layer; this is the pillar-not-formatter ceiling being reached.

The ginseng: plant a seed early in a life (cheap, every life can engage), it matures over the WHOLE run, and the decision is never whether to engage but **when to harvest** — cash out now for moderate value, or hold for maximum value and risk the tribulation arriving first.
