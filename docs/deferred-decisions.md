## Design decisions — worth deciding before or during slice 9 (Act II)

1. **Aspect routes are lattice-locked.** Element Soul Aspects require a held lattice Seed, so sect-focused and pill-focused builds are structurally confined to Formless. **REFRAMED (review pass):** the lock is fine ONLY if sect and pill each own something equally identity-defining — otherwise they're not builds, they're flavors of "not-lattice," and breaking the lock would paper over the real gap. Before acting on the cluster ratio, name the counter-monopolies. Working proposal to test against the run: **lattice owns WHO you are** (aspect, identity), **sect owns STANDING** (institutional multipliers, arsenal, deeds), **alchemy owns WHEN** (timing control — burst windows, breakthrough amplification, tribulation insurance). The diversity run now attributes sect/pill lag to the Formless penalty specifically (counterfactual probes) so we don't break the lock chasing the wrong cause.

2. **Keep-rule topology gaps (found by the competent-actor sim).** No keep rule ever protects `c` (Core Formation climb), and the `foundationSurvivesNascentSoul` keep is immediately re-wiped by each cycle's own c-climbs — so late-game play re-climbs c forever. ~30% of the competent actor's n-climb time is this churn. Decide: add a c keep rule (e.g., n milestone), accept the churn as intended texture ("the core must be re-tempered each ascent"), or fold into Act II's keep design. *Lean: decide inside slice 9's keep-rule work, not before.* **REFRAMED (review pass):** treat as design opportunity, not cleanup. The 30% c-churn is a rationalized emergent mechanic; when slice 9 touches keeps, the choice is deliberate — DELETE the churn (add a c-keep; churn was tax) or make the re-temper FELT (visible hardening, compounding payoff; churn becomes ritual). Same code touch, opposite philosophies. Do not sleepwalk into the delete.

3. **Corruption threshold ratchet.** Crossed heart-demon thresholds never re-arm: bleed corruption to zero and the old trials don't re-fire; the ladder only ratchets upward (then repeats its final trial every +120). Intended reading: each demon is faced once, then only the deepest returns. Alternative: cyclical trials (re-arm on full bleed-out) for farmability. *Lean: current ratchet is correct — Dao Heart farming already exists via the repeat ladder; re-arming would make bleed-out a farming exploit.* **DECIDED: ratchet stays.** (But see #6 — the ratchet's farmability is now an input to Samsara's economy.)

4. **Tribulation banked-Qi fuel vs automation.** The preparedness pool's banked-Qi term rewards holding a large pile at trigger time, while auto-q-prestige (Automation Tier 1) continuously spends the pile. Currently self-resolving in practice (the maturity model rests when formed), but nothing *guarantees* automation never eats a tribulation bank. Carried from the TMT-era review as "acceptable v1, flag for design." *Lean: leave until a real player report.* **DECIDED: leave — with the reframe that if the guard is ever built, build it for FEEL (the game holds its breath while the tribulation is ready; automation pausing reads as reverence) and the bank-safety comes free.**

5. **"Qi gains from spending" expedition modifier.** The design doc's Inverted Spirit Land example needs spend-path hooks the engine doesn't have; v1 shipped an Insight-driven inversion instead. The idea is recorded in `secret-realm.ts`'s header as a modifier family (gain-from-spending, cost-from-time, progress-from-stillness) for future sites. *Lean: build the spend-hook plumbing when Act II adds its realm sites, not before.*

## Decisions scheduled for slice 10 (Samsara)

6. **Dao Heart stacks across reincarnation.** Currently life-scoped; lean was eternal. **REFRAMED (review pass, coupled to #3):** eternal + the repeat-ladder's farmability = unbounded meta-currency across lives. That is either the demonic fantasy fully expressed (unboundedness as that build's intended endgame) or the thing Samsara soft-caps across lives — but it is THE question Samsara's economy must answer explicitly, not a side effect to discover. The whole samsaric economy balances around this answer; decide it first in the slice-10 design, before any Samsara numbers are written.

7. **Seclusion rungs across reincarnation.** Shipped eternal by design intent ("QoL is never clawed back") — Samsara's implementation must honor it; recorded so the slice-10 implementer doesn't "helpfully" reset them. *Not really open — a confirmation checkpoint.*

8. **Chronicle / epitaph system** (horizons §2). **REFRAMED (review pass): bound to #11 as one narrative-spine design pass** (the chronicle makes the past legible; the veil makes the future illegible — duals of a single posture). And consciously scope-decided BEFORE building: the "data table + formatter" costing is the floor, and anchoring on it risks shipping a formatter where the ceiling is a pillar — the dynasty-history save is the design's most distinctive retention hook (the screenshot-a-tragic-biography moment the research pointed at). The Samsara slice must decide flavor-line vs. system explicitly; the cheap version forecloses the tall one.

## Strategic (timing matters more than the answer)

9. **Steam intent.** **DECIDED (review pass): soft yes — architect for it, commitment to launch deferred.** Rationale: the option value decays fastest of anything on this list; save-migration and achievement scaffolding are cheap to build in and expensive to retrofit, and Act II is about to multiply what would need migrating. Consequences now: (a) golden-save lineage harness in CI before slice 9 content lands (a save from each shipped version, loaded + ticked through migrations); (b) achievements modeled as a registry that can map to Steam achievements later; (c) settles #15 (README matters now) and #13 (the Realistic band is the number to polish).

10. **Rivals-as-weather** (horizons §3). **DECIDED (review pass): split at the cheap/expensive seam.** Ambient rivals ship (another cultivator's tribulation lights the horizon, a breakthrough shakes the sky — no interaction; most of the inhabited-world payoff at a fraction of the cost); interactive rivals (contested slots/resources) are a v2 pillar, not built now. Architect the ambient events so interaction could hook in later. Xianxia is among the most social fantasy genres — pure solitude leaves its biggest hook on the table, but the interactive version balloons scope.

11. **Knowledge-veil / ancestral knowledge** (Wes's planned system). **RECUT (review pass): the axis is not aggressive-vs-gentle timing — it is WHAT gets veiled. Veil the AHEAD, never the NOW.** Grades are the language the optimizer thinks in; hiding the current loop's grades withholds the player's own controls and reads as hiding the ball (incremental players are optimizers first). Veiling is powerful on flavor-critical, optimization-optional information: the nature of unreached realms, the names of things not yet touched, what Spirit Severing is actually like. Mystify the horizon; keep the current loop fully legible. Bound to #8 in the narrative-spine pass.
