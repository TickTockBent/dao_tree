# The Third Corpse Speaks

Flavor draft (2026-07-03, kept by Wes). Candidate source material for the
realm-x journal when the third corpse ships — the severance mechanic says
this in numbers; the game might say it in words once, quietly, and never
again. Not wired to any JournalEntryKey yet; adapt freely when it lands.

The Corpse of the Past went quietly. It had been waiting a long time, and
the things it held — the aspect you chose before you knew what choosing
was — had grown thin as old paper. You cut, and the weakness that followed
felt honest, like the ache after setting a broken bone.

The Corpse of the Present fought. Of course it fought; it was the one
doing the fighting. Every pill in your satchel argued its case, every
meridian you'd opened at such cost sang its own name. You cut anyway,
and for six turnings of the ritual you were less than you had been, and
you did the work of the mountain regardless, and that — the doing of it
while diminished — was the severance. The knife was just punctuation.

But the Corpse of the Future is the strange one, and no one warns you,
because the ones who reached it stopped writing things down.

It doesn't hold anything you have. It holds everything you were going
to be. The manifestation you finally earned, tier by tier, the one the
lattice promised back when it was only a glimpse — the Future wears it
like a garment already tailored, and asks, without malice:

"If you cut me, who arrives at the peak?"

And the answer the ritual teaches — twelve steps, breakeven at seven,
the arithmetic of grief — is: someone does. Someone always arrives.
The multiplier ramps back past what was lost not because the universe
is kind but because you kept climbing while it wasn't. The self that
arrives is not the self that was promised. It is the self that was
practiced.

The old texts call the third cut "severing the future." The ones who
made it call it something smaller. They call it Tuesday: the day you
stopped waiting to become someone, and noticed you already had —
noticed it the way you notice breath, which is to say, by continuing.
