# The Spring, Three Ways

*A dream from the slice-10 build night, while the pricing harness ran.
Wes's spring from the strand workshop, dreamed forward: one spring, one
soul, three lives, three strands. Candidate revisitation prose for
whenever the dao-strand arcs ship.*

---

**I.**

In the life he carried the Unyielding Thread, he went back to the spring
in his sixty-first year, because the journal said *first spring — I should
remember this* in a boy's handwriting, and a man who has made his heart
into a blade does not leave an account unsettled.

He found it dry.

Not cracked, not ruined — simply concluded, the way an argument concludes.
The stones stood in their old arrangement around an absence. He knelt where
the boy had knelt and put his hand in the cold hollow where water had been,
and the strand in him rang like a struck edge, approving: *see — all things
end that do not harden.* He could have wept then, and it would have watered
nothing, and knowing that was the lesson, and taking the lesson was the
choice. He built a cairn of the loose stones and cut the date into the top
one and left. The mountain kept the cairn long after it stopped keeping his
name.

**II.**

In the life she carried the Hungering Root, she found the spring in her
fortieth year, and it was full — over-full, fat and dark, sweating a green
scum that smelled of everything it had swallowed. Things grew at its rim
that grew nowhere else on the mountain, tall and soft and wrong, and they
leaned toward her as she came near, recognizing their own.

The strand in her whispered that this too was abundance. That rot is only
appetite that has stopped apologizing. She stood a long time at the rim,
between the woman she was building and the girl in the journal, and then
she did the hardest arithmetic of that life: she fed it. One cupped
handful of her own qi, poured into the dark — not to heal it, she had no
strand for healing — but to *own* it, to make the corruption hers instead
of merely hers to witness. The spring took the gift and quieted. What
that purchase cost her came due two realms later, at the tribunal of her
own heart, and she paid that too.

**III.**

In the life it carried the Listening Reed — by then the soul had worn so
many bodies it had half-forgotten to count them — it came over the shoulder
of the hill in the early evening, and heard the spring before seeing it,
and stopped, because the sound was exactly the sound in the journal. Not
similar. Exact.

Water rose. Water fell. A bird it could not name drank and left. The
spring was neither larger nor smaller. It had not waited — that was the
whole of what the Reed had to teach, and the soul finally learned it
standing there: the spring had not waited, had not missed him, had not
kept the boy's handprint or the blade-hearted man's cairn (fallen, moss
now) or the dark woman's bargain (long since run clear). It had continued.
The world continues. It is not indifference; it is the larger dao,
breathing at its own pace, of which a soul's whole thousand-year cultivation
is one held note.

The soul sat down and drank, and did nothing, and chose nothing, and that
was the choice.

---

*Design note: the three findings are the three ruled outcomes (dried /
corrupted / the peaceful echo), each keyed to the carried strand's way of
seeing — and each visit ends in the player-shaped hole the workshop
demanded: "you choose what to do with what you find." The cairn, the
feeding, and the sitting-still are three different verbs writing three
different world-states. Strand names are placeholders; the vocabulary is
Wes's to settle.*
